
# Blocksize War  

### 1, 2 First Strike & March to War   

> Bier, Jonathan. Bier, Jonathan-PCggXe - The Blocksize War_ The battle for control over Bitcoins protocol rulesThe Blocksize War_ The Battle For Control (2021) - libgen.li (Function). Kindle Edition.  

It is not possible to cover every **twist and turn** in the **labyrinthine** conflict or all the arguments, but I have provided a **chronology** of the most significant events.  

For convenience, in this book the two sides are labeled “big blockers” and “small blockers”, although in reality this is an **oversimplification** and the groups were not **homogenous**.  

As an open system, it was not possible to **conceal** this disagreement from the public any longer.  

The debate on Reddit and BitcoinTalk had been intensifying for a while, but the launch of Bitcoin XT really accelerated the **vitriolic** nature of the argument.  

On August 17, 2015, two days after the official launch of Bitcoin XT, Theymos announced a new Reddit moderation policy. This policy proved to be both extremely controversial and divisive.  

The result of this moderation policy was a bifurcation of the Bitcoin community. Larger blockers gradually moved over to an alternative Bitcoin subreddit, /r/btc.  

At this point, the debate appears to get almost religious, with both sides poring over every Satoshi quote looking for comments or interpretations supporting their cause.  

Bitcoin had to be robust in of itself to withstand the immense pressures it would be exposed to as a controversial and revolutionary money system, without reliance on one individual, who could presumably easily be stopped or could vanish at any time.
