**Grungy**  
   - **Definition**: Dirty, shabby, or unkempt in appearance; also used to describe a style in music or fashion that embraces a messy or unrefined aesthetic.  
   - **Common Usage**: Commonly used to describe a style, especially in fashion and music, or to convey a lack of cleanliness.  
   - **Examples**:  
     - She wore a grungy t-shirt and ripped jeans for the concert, fitting right in with the crowd.  
   - **Alternatives**: Scruffy, shabby, disheveled.  

---  

**Treacly**  
   - **Definition**: Excessively sweet or sentimental; overly sugary in tone, often to the point of being cloying.  
   - **Common Usage**: Less common in daily conversation, but often used in literary critiques or discussions about movies and songs.  
   - **Examples**:  
     - The movie's treacly ending left many viewers rolling their eyes at its predictability.  
   - **Alternatives**: Cloying, mushy, sentimental.  

---  

**Slapdash**  
   - **Definition**: Done hastily and carelessly; characterized by a lack of attention to detail.  
   - **Common Usage**: Fairly common in informal contexts when describing poor workmanship or haphazard actions.  
   - **Examples**:  
     - The contractor’s slapdash work resulted in a number of issues that had to be fixed later.  
   - **Alternatives**: Hasty, careless, superficial.  

---  

**Imbroglio**  

*   **Definition**: An extremely confused, complicated, or embarrassing situation. It often refers to a public entanglement or a messy dispute.  
*   **Common Usage**: Less common in daily conversation. You're more likely to encounter it in news headlines, political commentary, literary works, or when describing complex and messy public affairs or personal conflicts.  
*   **Examples**:  
    *   The company found itself in an imbroglio after several top executives were implicated in a financial scandal.  
    *   Their holiday started off fine, but quickly devolved into an imbroglio involving lost luggage, a hotel booking mix-up, and a language barrier.  
*   **Alternatives**: Mess, **predicament**, entanglement, **quagmire**, kerfuffle (more informal).  

---  

**Abrogate**  

*   **Definition**: To repeal or do away with (a law, right, or formal agreement); to evade (a responsibility or duty).  
*   **Common Usage**: Not commonly used in daily life. It's a more formal, legal, or political term.  
*   **Examples**:  
    *   The government decided to abrogate the outdated treaty.  
    *   He tried to abrogate his responsibilities as a parent, but the court wouldn't allow it.  
*   **Alternatives**: Repeal, revoke, annul, cancel, nullify, repudiate.
